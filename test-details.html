Complete Blood Count (CBC)

Comprehensive blood analysis for overall health assessment

Test Overview

The Complete Blood Count (CBC) is one of the most commonly ordered blood tests. It provides valuable information about the types and numbers of cells in your blood, including red blood cells, white blood cells, and platelets. This test helps evaluate your overall health and detect a wide range of disorders.

Why It's Done

A CBC test is often part of a routine health checkup. It can help diagnose conditions such as anemia, infections, inflammation, bleeding disorders, and blood cancers. Your doctor may also order this test to monitor an existing condition or to check how treatments are affecting your blood cells.

Understanding Your Results

Your CBC results will show the count and characteristics of different blood cells. Normal ranges can vary slightly based on age, sex, and other factors. Your doctor will interpret the results in the context of your overall health, symptoms, and medical history. Abnormal results may indicate various conditions that require further investigation or treatment.

It's important to discuss your results with your healthcare provider, who can explain what they mean for your specific situation and recommend any necessary follow-up tests or treatments.
